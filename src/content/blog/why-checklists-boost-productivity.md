Checklists turn big, fuzzy tasks into small, clear steps you can complete one by one. That single shift does something powerful: it frees up your working memory.

## They reduce mental load

When everything lives in your head, your brain works overtime just to remember. Writing it down lets you focus on *doing* instead of *remembering*.

## They prevent mistakes

From pilots to surgeons, professionals rely on checklists because they catch the small steps we skip when we're busy or tired.

## How to build a checklist you'll use

1. Keep it short — only what matters.
2. Use action verbs (“Send…”, “Check…”).
3. Make it reusable for repeating tasks.
4. Review and trim it regularly.
